# Shepherd Verses — Brand Guide

## 🕯️ Brand Essence

**Name:** Shepherd Verses  
**Tagline:** "Daily Bible verses and affirmations to guide your heart through every emotion"  
**Mission:** Provide spiritual comfort, hope, and guidance through personalized scripture delivery

## 🎨 Visual Identity

### Color Palette

```
Primary Background (cream):        #F2F1EE
Primary Text (dark blue-grey):     #3A4A5A
Secondary Text (medium blue-grey): #4A5B6B
Accent Light (soft blue):          #A9C3D6
Accent Warm (soft taupe):          #E7DED2
Accent Warm Dark (terracotta):     #D88C7A
```

**Color Psychology:**
- Warm cream (#F2F1EE): Safe, calm, approachable
- Dark blue-grey (#3A4A5A): Trustworthy, spiritual, grounded
- Soft blue (#A9C3D6): Peace, serenity, clarity
- Terracotta (#D88C7A): Warmth, comfort, spiritual earth

### Typography

**Headings:** Lora (serif)
- Weight: 400-700 (regular to bold)
- Use for: Page titles, section headers, emotion names
- Feeling: Elegant, spiritual, timeless
- Examples: "Welcome to Shepherd Verses", "How It Works"

**Body Text:** Inter (sans-serif)
- Weight: 400-500 (regular to medium)
- Size: 14-16px
- Use for: Copy, descriptions, buttons
- Feeling: Clean, modern, readable
- Line height: 1.6 (spacious, calm)

**Accent Text:** Lora for spiritual quotes, scripture references

### Visual Style

- **Aesthetic:** Minimal, clean, serene
- **Spacing:** Generous white space (breathing room)
- **Imagery:** Nature, light, spiritual elements
- **Icons:** Simple, line-based, symbolic
- **Animations:** Subtle, calming (fades, gentle slides)
- **Cards/Sections:** Rounded corners (12-16px), soft shadows

## 💬 Voice & Tone

### How We Speak

**Tone:**
- Peaceful, never rushed
- Comforting, never patronizing
- Spiritual, never preachy
- Hopeful, never toxic positivity
- Warm, never cold

**Examples:**

✅ **Good:** "Take a moment with scripture that speaks to your heart right now"  
❌ **Bad:** "BOOST YOUR MOOD WITH BIBLE VERSES!"

✅ **Good:** "You're not alone in how you're feeling. Scripture is here for you."  
❌ **Bad:** "Stop being sad! Here's a verse!"

✅ **Good:** "What's on your heart today?"  
❌ **Bad:** "Pick an emotion below"

### Language Principles

1. **Use "you/your"** — Personal, intimate
2. **Active, gentle verbs** — "Share", "explore", "discover" (not "consume", "get")
3. **Spiritual language** — "Scripture", "affirmation", "spirit", "peace" (not "content", "product")
4. **Questions over statements** — Invite reflection, not commands
5. **Short sentences** — Breathing room for contemplation

## 📱 User Experience

### Emotional Journey

**Landing:**
- User feels: Curiosity, openness, hope
- Message: "This is a safe space for your emotions"

**Signup:**
- User feels: Trust, ease, simplicity
- Message: "Three simple steps, then peace"

**Welcome:**
- User feels: Confirmation, guidance, readiness
- Message: "Your journey begins here"

**Daily Message:**
- User feels: Seen, supported, hopeful
- Message: "Scripture meets you where you are"

### Design Principles

1. **Calmness First** — Every element serves peace, not distraction
2. **Clarity** — No confusion about what to do next
3. **Spirituality** — Design should feel sacred, not commercial
4. **Accessibility** — Large text, high contrast, simple navigation
5. **Consistency** — Same experience across web, Telegram, future platforms

## 📢 Social Media Tone

### Content Pillars

1. **Scripture of the Day** — Share one verse with context
2. **Affirmation** — Daily mantra for reflection
3. **Emotion Story** — "How to handle [emotion] with faith"
4. **User Stories** — Testimonials, real experiences
5. **Spiritual Teaching** — Gentle wisdom, biblical context

### Posting Style

- **Short, punchy** — Captions under 150 words
- **Question-based** — "What emotion are you feeling today?"
- **Visual-first** — Beautiful imagery + minimal text
- **Authentic** — Real stories, not polished marketing
- **Call-to-action:** "Download the app" or "Join our community"

## 🎯 Brand Don'ts

❌ Don't use corporate jargon ("maximize", "leverage", "synergy")  
❌ Don't be overly cheerful or "toxic positive"  
❌ Don't compare to secular meditation apps  
❌ Don't push sales hard — let peace sell itself  
❌ Don't use religious guilt or shame  
❌ Don't make it feel like a church service  
❌ Don't use outdated religious imagery  
❌ Don't oversimplify mental health struggles

## 🌟 Brand Guardians (For Future Team)

When in doubt, ask:
1. "Does this feel peaceful?"
2. "Would Gareth's grandmother feel welcomed here?"
3. "Is this authentic, not just marketing?"
4. "Does it serve the user's spiritual peace first, revenue second?"

If the answer to all 4 is yes → proceed.
